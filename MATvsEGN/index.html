Trends in Enrollment for Select Mathematics and Engineering courses

Data is taken from scrapes from each semester of all courses listed in SeaNet, and values were gathered near the UNCW census date. Therefore, while these are publicly available and can be checked, they may differ from other sources for enrollment and credit hour production, but such differences will be minor.


In the graphs below, we revisit MAT 275 together with the 300-level required math courses, followed by the three calculus courses. Note: MAT 361 is a required course for the BS Coastal Engineering, as are all three calculus courses.



The graph below considers enrollment in MAT 261 and 361 together with three engineering courses; EGN 310, 330, & 340 that have one of those math courses as a stated prerequisite. This graph is included to give an overall picture of enrollments. (MAT 162 is not a direct prerequisite for any specific engineering course, and MAT 161 is a direct prerequisite only for ENG 102, neither is considered here)


To compare trends, it is not optimal to compare an engineering course enrollment to its math prerequisite in the same year. The next chart modifies the previous one to match math enrollments a year ahead of engineering enrollments--so, the year of the math courses is on the top horizontal axis, engineering on the bottom (which is done in all subsequent graphs). Hues are matched for engineering courses and their math prerequisites.


In the next chart, we make it even easier to compare trends, by translating enrollments (but keeping the same scale), so there are two different vertical axes with differing ranges but having the same step size.
