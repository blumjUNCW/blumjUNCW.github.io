Trends in Enrollment and Credit Hour Production

Data is taken from scrapes of courses listed in SeaNet, and values were gathered near the UNCW census date. Therefore, these may differ from other sources for enrollment and credit hour production, but such differences will be minor.


This first chart considers student credit hours generated in upper-level math courses. Upper level math is defined as 300 level or higher for this case. Student credit hours are used to ensure non-standard enrollments are weighed properly when pooled with standard 3-hour courses. Timing of the onset of various factors is annotated as well.




Most of the time, the non-standard enrollments are of little consequence for total hours generated. In this chart, only enrollment in 3-credit courses are considered. Also, as MAT 495 is the only 400-level course to be required (outside of an internship or honors thesis) in all versions/tracks of the undergraduate math degrees, it is set out on its own.
